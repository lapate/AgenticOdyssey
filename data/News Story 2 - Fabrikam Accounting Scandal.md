# EXCLUSIVE: Fabrikam, Inc. Rocked by Accounting Scandal — Zava's Largest Customer Under Federal Investigation

**ZavaTimes Investigative Report** | *February 26, 2026*

---

## SEC and DOJ Launch Joint Probe Into Fabrikam's Reported Revenues

In a bombshell revelation that sent shockwaves through the athletic apparel supply chain, federal regulators announced Wednesday that **Fabrikam, Inc.** — the East region manufacturing giant and Zava's single largest B2B customer — is under investigation for allegedly inflating purchase orders and fabricating inventory records over a two-year period.

The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and the Department of Justice (DOJ) have jointly filed preliminary injunctions freezing portions of Fabrikam's corporate accounts, raising immediate questions about the company's ability to honor outstanding payables — including an estimated **$1.08 million in cumulative business with Zava**.

## What This Means for Zava

Fabrikam has been a cornerstone of Zava's East region business strategy. With **37 completed orders**, the company has been the top purchaser of:

- **ZavaCore Fiber — Field Grade** raw materials (at $850/unit, orders typically in quantities of 30–50 units)
- **Systems Custom Team Jerseys** for Fabrikam's branded retail network
- **ZavaCore Fiber — Nano Grade**, the ultra-premium material at $2,293/unit used in Fabrikam's high-end product lines

Industry sources suggest Fabrikam may have been **double-booking purchase orders** — recording materials as received from Zava and other suppliers to inflate its own asset valuations, while actual inventory sat in third-party warehouses or, in some cases, was never ordered at all.

> *"If even half of these allegations are substantiated, we're looking at one of the largest B2B fraud cases in the athletic materials sector in a decade,"* said Dr. Mariana Voss, a forensic accounting professor at Columbia Business School.

## Ripple Effects Across the East Region

The fallout extends well beyond Fabrikam's walls. **VanArsdel, Ltd.** and **Adatum Corporation** — both East region Zava customers — have disclosed that they subcontract portions of their manufacturing to Fabrikam. VanArsdel ($544,490 in Zava revenue) has paused new orders while conducting an internal review of its supply chain exposure.

**Bellows College**, which sources custom team uniforms through a consortium that includes Fabrikam as a co-manufacturer, has frozen its upcoming $31,000+ jersey order pending clarification from Zava on alternative production partners.

Collectively, the East region's four major B2B accounts — Fabrikam, VanArsdel, Bellows College, and Adatum — represent over **$2.36 million in Zava revenue**. If order volumes contract by even 20%, the quarterly impact could approach **$120,000 in lost gross profit**.

## Zava Moves to Contain Damage

In an internal memo obtained by ZavaTimes, Zava's Chief Revenue Officer outlined a three-pronged response:

1. **Credit hold** on all pending Fabrikam shipments until federal guidance is clarified
2. **Accelerated outreach** to West region partners — particularly **Contoso, Ltd.** ($849K in revenue) and **Adventure Works Cycles** ($438K) — to absorb displaced production capacity
3. **Diversification initiative** to reduce single-customer concentration, with a target of no single B2B account exceeding 12% of regional revenue

## Market Reaction

Zava's management issued a brief statement: *"We maintain rigorous credit controls and are confident in the integrity of our own financial reporting. We are cooperating fully with investigators and have engaged outside counsel to protect Zava's interests."*

The broader athletic apparel sector index fell 1.8% on the news, with investors reassessing counterparty risk across supply chains that rely on concentrated B2B relationships.
