**ZavaTimes Market Report** | *February 27, 2026*

---

## Bay Area's "Corporate Wellness Mandate" Sparks Unprecedented Orders

A sweeping new corporate wellness trend originating in Silicon Valley is driving a **massive surge in demand** for Zava products across the West region — and the company is scrambling to keep up.

It started when three of California's largest tech firms announced mandatory "Active Culture" workplace programs in January 2026, requiring employees to participate in company-sponsored athletic leagues, cycling clubs, and fitness challenges. The movement has since spread to over 40 mid-size companies across the West Coast, and Zava — with its premium athletic performance line and cutting-edge **Smart Cleats** technology — has found itself at the center of the frenzy.

Now, with the wellness boom in full swing, all of them want more — and they want it *yesterday*.

## Smart Cleats Pro: The Surprise Breakout Star

Perhaps no product has benefited more than the **Smart Cleats Pro** ($249/pair), which has become a status symbol among West Coast tech workers. With built-in performance sensors and Zava's proprietary fiber technology, the Smart Cleats have generated **$578,987 in revenue** across **224 orders and 2,512 units sold** — and demand is accelerating.

**Adventure Works Cycles** alone has placed an emergency bulk order of 500 pairs for a corporate cycling league partnership, while **Contoso, Ltd.** is requesting exclusive color variants for its employee wellness program.

> *"We've never seen anything like it,"* said a Zava West region sales director. *"Our Smart Cleats inventory was planned for a 6-month sell-through. We're on track to sell out in 6 weeks."*

## Supply Chain Strain

The boom is creating a double-edged sword. **Relecloud** and **Boulder Innovations** — Zava's primary B2B buyers of **ZavaCore Fiber** raw materials — have increased their combined order volumes by 35% to feed downstream manufacturing. But Zava's fiber production capacity, which supplies the proprietary materials used in nearly every product line, is approaching its ceiling.

The **ZavaCore Fiber — Systems Grade** ($1,250/unit) and **Nano Grade** ($2,293/unit) variants are both on allocation, with lead times stretching from the typical 20 days to nearly 45 days. **Trey Research**, which relies on ZavaCore materials for its R&D collaborations, has flagged that delayed shipments could jeopardize a joint product development timeline.

## eCommerce Surge Adds Pressure

The Direct eCommerce channel — Zava's largest B2C outlet with **1,223 orders and $322,550 in revenue** — is seeing a 60% spike in West region web traffic as individual consumers join the wellness trend. Top sellers include:

- **Field Premium Performance Leggings** — $439,907 total revenue, now backordered in 3 sizes
- **Field Elite Short Sleeve Top** — waitlisted on the Zava website
- **Smart Insoles** ($59/pair) — selling 4x projected volume as a gateway product

## What Comes Next

Zava leadership has announced an **emergency capacity expansion** at its West Coast fulfillment center and is negotiating expedited raw material contracts with offshore fiber suppliers to supplement domestic production. The company is also exploring a **priority allocation program** for its top West region B2B accounts to prevent order cancellations.

Analysts are bullish: *"This is the kind of organic demand surge that brands dream about,"* wrote Morgan & Associates in a morning note. *"If Zava can scale supply to meet it, the West region alone could drive 15–20% year-over-year growth."*

The question is whether Zava's supply chain can keep pace with its suddenly red-hot brand.
